For Applied Project 2017

Corporate Travel Management Solution

Client: Canadian Commercial Corportation

ROLE: Lead User Experience Designer

Our Client

A Crown corporation of the Government of Canada, the Canadian Commercial Corporation (CCC) acts as Canada’s international contracting and procurement agency. Their mandate is “to assist in the development of trade between Canada and other nations and to assist persons in Canada to obtain goods or commodities from outside Canada, and to dispose of goods and commodities that are available for export from Canada."

My Role as Lead User Experience Designer

  • Responsible for the overall strategy and user experience
  • Was in charge of designers and their work
  • Conducted Contextual Interviews with the users
  • Created the user flows for the mobile and the website
  • Sketched out interactions specs, and user flows specifically for the website portion
  • Create Wireframes, high-fi mockups, low-fi to high-fi prototypes for the Website
  • UI Design for the Website
  • Front-End Developer for the Mobile version

The Challenge and Ideation

The goal of the project was to address the “paper problem” that the CCC is facing. The CCC lacked an electronic corporate travel system, all travel and associated processes were paper based

User Research

Conducted contextual interviews with 5 users. 2 traveler, 1 auditor, and 2 traveler arrangers. Found that they have a major issue with expense tracking on paper. Our findings were that their process for asking permission and submitting forms is not working well.

Skectching & Wireframes

Sketched out ideas of potential solutions on paper and had more than 4 iterations of the skteches. Concept tested the sketches after every iteration

Mobile and Web Prototype

Created a high-fidelity prototype in Adobe XD and colour was added by an Visual Designer. The solution will be usability testing in the near future.

Solution

The solution was to create a Mobile Application and a website. The solution has placed 3rd in Algonquin College's Applied Research .
